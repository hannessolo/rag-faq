Oaty, chocolatey goodness. With its cake-like consistency and chocolate-filled sweetness, these high protein baked oats an absolute winner for a killer breakfast, or cosy evening snack. 

Made using [protein powder](https://www.bulk.com/uk/protein) and peanut butter, this protein baked oats recipe has great macros. Ideal for [pre-workout](https://www.bulk.com/uk/sports-nutrition/pre-workout) fueller, or post-workout recovery. See the ingredients and method below.

**WHAT ARE THE MACROS AND CALORIES FOR PROTEIN BAKED OATS?**
------------------------------------------------------------

**Nutritional breakdown (estimated per serving)**

*   Calories: 442 kcals
*   Protein: 21g
*   Fat: 16g
*   Carbs: 59g
*   Fibre: 6g

**WHAT INGREDIENTS DO YOU NEED FOR PROTEIN** **BAKED OATS****?**
----------------------------------------------------------------

*   45g porridge oats
*   2 tbsp **Bulk™** [vanilla protein powder](https://www.bulk.com/uk/protein/vanilla-protein-powders)
*   ¼ tsp baking powder
*   ½ banana, mashed
*   1 tbsp **Bulk™** smooth [Peanut Butter](https://www.bulk.com/uk/products/peanut-butter-1kg/bpf-pbut)
*   80ml oat drink
*   1 tbsp [Zero Calorie Syrup™](https://www.bulk.com/uk/products/zero-calorie-syrup/bpf-zcsy)
*   1 tbsp chocolate chips

**HOW DO YOU MAKE HIGH** **PROTEIN BAKED OATS****?**
----------------------------------------------------

1.  Preheat oven to 180C/160C fan.
2.  Mix the oats, protein powder and baking powder in a bowl, then add the mashed banana, peanut butter, zero-calorie syrup and milk. Mix to combine.
3.  Transfer into a ramekin dish and top with a sprinkle of chocolate chips. 
4.  Bake for 20-25 minutes. 

**HOW MANY PEOPLE DOES THIS SERVE?**
------------------------------------

This serves one person. Double it to share with a friend (or not).
