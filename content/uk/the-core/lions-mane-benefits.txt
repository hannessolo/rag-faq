**What is Lion’s Mane?** 
-------------------------

Lion’s mane mushrooms are large, white fungi that resemble a lion’s mane. Though often considered a single type of mushroom, there are actually three species, with Hericium erinaceus being the most commonly available.

These mushrooms are packed with vitamins such as thiamine, riboflavin, and niacin, and are also a good source of essential minerals like manganese, zinc, and potassium.

**What is Lion’s Mane Good For (according to early studies)?**

While research on lion’s mane is still in its early stages, initial studies suggest several potential benefits.

**However, these findings are _not conclusive_ and should be interpreted with due diligence.**

Some studies indicate that lion’s mane mushrooms may alleviate symptoms of depression and anxiety.

For example, one study suggests that lion’s mane “may improve speed of performance and exert a stress-reduction effect.”

Lion’s mane mushrooms might aid in improving cognitive function, particularly in older adults, potentially boosting memory.
